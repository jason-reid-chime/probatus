# Probatus — Go-to-Market Plan

---

## Strategy

**Start with compliance pain, not software features.** The single best buying trigger for a calibration company is an upcoming accreditation audit — specifically a SCC (Standards Council of Canada) or A2LA surveillance audit against ISO/IEC 17025. Companies scrambling to produce complete, traceable records in a hurry will pay to solve that problem immediately. A secondary trigger is losing a contract because a customer required digital certificates or a web portal for certificate lookup and the company couldn't deliver.

Your outreach should lead with one of these two scenarios, not with "modernize your workflow." For a bootstrapped solo founder, the most efficient sales motion is direct outreach to the owner or lab manager — these companies are almost never enterprise-buying-committee situations. One person decides. Start in Southern Ontario where you already have geographic and cultural context, get two or three paying reference customers, then expand. Attend or sponsor Measurement Canada industry events and ASQ (American Society for Quality) local section meetings — calibration lab owners talk to each other, and a peer reference in the same city closes deals that cold email never will.

**Overcoming "we've always done it this way"** requires repositioning the risk. The objection is really "change is risky and our current system works well enough." Flip it: paper and Access are the risk. A single failed audit finding citing incomplete records, a technician losing a field notebook, or a customer demanding a digital audit trail you can't produce — any of those can cost more than Probatus in a single week. Offer a 60-day pilot with their real jobs and real instruments. No data migration required. Let them run paper in parallel if they want. The goal of the pilot is to get one field technician producing one PDF certificate from the app — once that happens, the objection is gone. Ask for a case study in exchange for a discounted pilot rate.

**Pricing model:** Charge per active technician per month — this aligns your revenue with the customer's growth and is the simplest metric for a lab owner to understand.

| Tier | Price |
|------|-------|
| Per technician/month (annual billing) | $49–79 USD |
| Minimum seats | 3 |
| Lab base fee (unlimited certs, instruments, customers) | $99 USD/month |
| Annual prepay discount | 20% |

A 5-technician shop lands at roughly $350–500/month — a fraction of one day's billable field time. Do not charge per certificate — calibration companies produce hundreds per month and metered output destroys adoption.

## Cold Outreach Script

**Email subject:** `ISO 17025 audit prep — how are you handling your field records?`

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm building Probatus specifically for calibration service companies — it replaces paper field sheets and MS Access with a mobile-first app that produces signed PDF certificates on-site, works offline in the field, and keeps a complete audit trail your assessor can review without you spending two days printing binders.
>
> ISO/IEC 17025 Clause 7.5 (technical records) and Clause 7.8 (reporting) are the two areas where paper-based shops most often get findings — Probatus addresses both by design, with immutable record timestamps and certificate version control built in.
>
> Would a 20-minute call be worth it to see whether it fits how [Company] works? Happy to show a live demo with pressure or temperature calibration workflows — whichever is closest to your bread and butter.
>
> — Jason Reid, Probatus
> [phone number]

*Keep the signature minimal. Do not attach anything to the first email.*

## Talking Points by Objection

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We've always used Access" | "Access doesn't work offline in the field, doesn't produce a signed PDF, and doesn't have an immutable audit trail. One audit finding costs more than a year of Probatus." |
| "We're too small for software" | "Probatus starts at 3 seats, ~$350/month. That's less than one hour of billable time. And it saves your techs 20–30 minutes per job on paperwork." |
| "We'd have to migrate all our data" | "You don't. Start with new jobs. Your old Access records stay exactly where they are. Migrate at your pace or not at all." |
| "Our customers don't need digital certs" | "They will. Municipalities and pharma customers are increasingly requiring digital audit trails. Get ahead of it before you lose a contract." |
| "IT won't approve cloud software" | "Probatus works offline-first — your data is on the device until you sync. And Supabase is SOC 2 compliant." |
